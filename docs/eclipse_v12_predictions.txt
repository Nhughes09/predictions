## WHY V9 PREDICTIONS WERE RETIRED

Critical error found in V9: Eskdalemuir (ESK) and Lerwick (LER) were
assigned coverage fractions of 0.98 and 0.92 respectively. This is wrong.
The August 12, 2026 eclipse path crosses SPAIN and Iceland — NOT Scotland.
ESK (55.3°N) and LER (60.1°N) are well outside the totality path.
Correct coverage: ESK ~0.55, LER ~0.42.

This error inflated the predicted signals at Scottish stations significantly.
V12 corrects coverage fractions for all stations from eclipse path data.
V12 also adds 4 new stations and applies H(r)/r field strength scaling.

## MODEL-DISCRIMINATING PREDICTIONS

### E-PRED-A: PEAK TRACKS ECLIPSE GEOMETRY, NOT SOLAR NOON
Signal peak at each station occurs at local eclipse maximum
(greatest shadow coverage at that station), NOT at solar noon.
Globe model: no mechanism for eclipse magnetic effect.
Dome model: shadow blocks ionospheric current → aetheric field disturbance.
Falsification: peaks systematically at solar noon rather than eclipse max.

### E-PRED-B: SIGNAL SCALES WITH COVERAGE × FIELD STRENGTH
Hartland (cov=0.80, FSF=1.47) should show LARGER signal than
Ebro (cov=0.95, FSF=1.03) despite lower coverage — because field
strength factor dominates. Predicted: HAD=-12.8 nT vs EBR=-10.7 nT.
This specific cross-station ordering is a direct V12 prediction.
Falsification: HAD signal smaller than EBR signal.

### E-PRED-C: SG GRAVIMETERS REGISTER 0.0 µGal
European superconducting gravimeters (Membach, Strasbourg, Vienna)
show NO gravitational signal during eclipse.
Confirms: mechanism is electromagnetic-aetheric, NOT gravitational.
Replicates WIN-013 (Membach 1999) and WIN-014 (China network 2010).
Falsification: measurable gravity anomaly detected at SG stations.

### E-PRED-D: NON-PATH STATIONS BELOW NOISE FLOOR
Stations with coverage < 0.25 (e.g. eastern Europe, Russia) show
signals below 2 nT noise floor — not attributable to eclipse.
Falsification: strong signal (>3 nT) at stations with <0.25 coverage.

## FIELD STRENGTH FACTOR EXPLANATION (V12)

In V12, the firmament height H varies with radial distance r from pole:
  H(r) = 8537 · exp(-r/8619) km

The aetheric field is more concentrated where the dome is tall relative
to the disc radius. Field density ~ H(r)/r.

At BOU (lat=40°, r=5423 km): H/r = 4553/5423 = 0.8396
At HAD (lat=51°, r=4232 km): H/r = 5225/4232 = 1.2345 → FSF = 1.471
At SNK (lat=28°, r=7021 km): H/r = 3780/7021 = 0.5384 → FSF = 0.642

This predicts that mid-latitude British stations (HAD, ESK, NGK) show
STRONGER signals per unit coverage than Spanish or Canary stations —
because they sit under a thicker aetheric layer.

This is a direct, testable V12 prediction. The V9 flat-H model
predicted signals proportional to coverage only. V12 adds the
field-density gradient. The two models make different station rankings.
